Accomodation

There are of course a great many places to stay in Ireland and you are welcome to stay at any of them. To help you narrow down your search we have listed some suggestions below that are both close to the venue and recommended. We will be running buses to Derry and locally to and from the venue. If you choose to stay further afield you may need to sort your own transport.

Derry

Derry is the closest City to the wedding venue and has an array of hotel options including:


The Bishop's Gate

The City Hotel

The Shipquay Hotel

The Maldron Hotel

Da Vinci's Ramada hotel

If you choose to stay in Derry we will be putting on a return bus service to and from the Wedding Venue to the Derry City Center.

Ballyliffin

If you would like to be closer to the Wedding Venue and stay in Ballyliffin, you once again you have a number of hotels and holiday home choices in the area including:


Ballyliffin Hotel

Strand Hotel

Ballyliffin Lodge and Spa Hotel

Ballyliffin TownHouse


Donegal Cottage Holdiays and AirBnB also have a number of holiday homes available in the area

Getting There

By plane:

The nearest airport is Derry Airport in Eglinton however it is a very small airport and they only have flights from a few places in the Uk; Glasgow, London-Stansted and Liverpool. There are a couple of car hire places at the airport including Hertz, Avis and Enterprise Rent-a- car however if you need a pick-up from the airport please don’t hesitate to ask either of us and we’ll be happy to arrange something for you.

Alternative airports are Belfast International Airport and George Best Belfast City Airport which are much bigger and have a good selection of routes. Car hire is also available at these airports as well as buses to Derry City.

By Ferry:

If you wish to bring your own car across on the ferry there are a couple of options offered by Stenaline and P&O ferries from Scotland and Liverpool to Belfast.

Driving:

It is a good idea to have a decent satnav or access to Google maps when driving from Belfast or Derry to Ballyliffin or Belfast to Derry as we are a fan of our winding country roads here in Ireland. Again, if you need any help please ask as we have relatives who have done the journey numerous times and would be happy to help with directions or pick-ups.
